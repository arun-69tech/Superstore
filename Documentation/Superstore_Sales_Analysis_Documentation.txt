Phase 2 : Python Data Cleaning
==============================

Step 1 : Remove Row ID

Command:

data.drop(columns="Row ID", inplace=True)

Finding:
The "Row ID" column was removed because it was a sequential
identifier and provided no analytical value.


Step 2 : Convert Postal Code to Text

Command:

data['Postal Code'] = data['Postal Code'].astype(str)

Finding:
Postal Code was converted from integer to string datatype
because postal codes are identifiers rather than numerical
values.

Step 3 : Convert Date Columns

Commands:

data['Order Date'] = pd.to_datetime(data['Order Date'],dayfirst=True)

data['Ship Date'] = pd.to_datetime(data['Ship Date'],dayfirst=True)

Finding:
Order Date and Ship Date were successfully converted from
object datatype to datetime datatype.

Step 4 : Create Shipping Duration

Command:

data['Shipping Duration'] = (data['Ship Date'] - data['Order Date']).dt.days

Finding:
A new feature called "Shipping Duration" was created to
represent the number of days taken for shipment.

Step 5 : Extract Date Components

Commands:

data['Order Year'] = data['Order Date'].dt.year

data['Order Month'] = data['Order Date'].dt.month_name()

data['Order Quarter'] = data['Order Date'].dt.quarter

Finding:
Three new columns were generated:

* Order Year
* Order Month
* Order Quarter

These features will support trend and seasonal analysis.

Step 6 : Verify Dataset Structure

Command:

data.info()

Finding:
Verification confirmed that:

* Row ID was removed.
* Postal Code became object datatype.
* Order Date and Ship Date became datetime datatype.
* Shipping Duration was added.
* Order Year, Order Month, and Order Quarter were added.

PHASE 4.1 : DAX MEASURES DOCUMENTATION

Objective:
The purpose of creating DAX measures is to transform 
raw transactional data into meaningful business KPIs 
that management can use for decision-making. 
These measures allow users to dynamically analyze 
the business under different filters and conditions.

------------------------------------
SECTION 1 : ESSENTIAL BUSINESS KPIs

1. Total Sales

DAX:
Total Sales = SUM('cleaned_superstore'[Sales])

Purpose:
Calculates the total revenue generated by the business.

Business Importance:
Acts as the primary indicator of overall business performance.

---

2. Total Profit

DAX:
Total Profit = SUM('cleaned_superstore'[Profit])

Purpose:
Calculates the total profit earned.

Business Importance:
Determines whether the business is financially healthy.

---

3. Total Orders

DAX:
Total Orders = DISTINCTCOUNT('cleaned_superstore'[Order ID])

Purpose:
Counts the number of unique orders placed.

Business Importance:
Measures transaction volume and customer purchasing activity.

---

4. Total Customers

DAX:
Total Customers = DISTINCTCOUNT('cleaned_superstore'[Customer ID])

Purpose:
Counts unique customers.

Business Importance:
Indicates the size of the customer base.

---

5. Total Quantity Sold

DAX:
Total Quantity Sold = SUM('cleaned_superstore'[Quantity])

Purpose:
Calculates total units sold.

Business Importance:
Represents sales volume and inventory movement.

---

6. Profit Margin %

DAX:
Profit Margin % = DIVIDE([Total Profit],[Total Sales],0)*100

Purpose:
Calculates the percentage of revenue converted into profit.

Business Importance:
Measures business efficiency and profitability.

Formula:
(Total Profit ÷ Total Sales) × 100

---

7. Average Order Value

DAX:
Average Order Value = DIVIDE([Total Sales],[Total Orders],0)

Purpose:
Determines the average revenue generated per order.

Business Importance:
Helps evaluate customer spending behavior.

Formula:
Total Sales ÷ Total Orders

---

8. Average Profit per Order

DAX:
Average Profit per Order = DIVIDE([Total Profit],[Total Orders],0)

Purpose:
Calculates profit earned from each order.

Business Importance:
Indicates the profitability of transactions.

---

9. Sales per Customer

DAX:
Sales per Customer = DIVIDE([Total Sales],[Total Customers],0)

Purpose:
Measures average revenue generated from each customer.

Business Importance:
Evaluates customer value.

---

10. Profit per Customer

DAX:
Profit per Customer = DIVIDE([Total Profit],[Total Customers],0)

Purpose:
Calculates average profit contribution per customer.

Business Importance:
Identifies customer profitability.

---

11. Average Shipping Days

DAX:
Average Shipping Days = AVERAGE('cleaned_superstore'[Shipping Duration])

Purpose:
Calculates the average delivery time.

Business Importance:
Monitors logistics efficiency and customer service quality.
